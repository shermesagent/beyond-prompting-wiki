# 05 — Practice

> Knowledge without action is trivia. This section turns the concepts into muscle memory — three weeks of exercises that take less than 10 minutes each.

This is where the operator→orchestrator shift stops being something you read about and starts being something you do. Each week builds on the last. Each exercise is small enough to fit into a coffee break. But the cumulative effect — if you actually do them — is real.

---

## The Three-Week Progression

| Week | Exercise | Time | What You'll Do |
|------|----------|------|----------------|
| 1 | [[Audit Your Prompts]] | 10 min | Look at your last 10 AI interactions and see what you're actually doing — and what you could be delegating |
| 2 | [[First Delegation]] | 10 min | Pick one recurring task and design a real agent workflow for it |
| 3 | [[Build a Tiny Pipeline]] | 10 min | Chain two tasks together and watch them run without you |

## Ongoing Practice

After the three-week progression, [[The Daily Standup]] is your maintenance habit — 5 minutes a day to review what ran, what worked, and what needs adjusting. It's the feedback loop that keeps your pipelines healthy and your delegation muscles sharp.

### Week 1 — Audit
Before you change anything, you need to see what you're actually doing. Most people have no idea what patterns live in their chat history. Week 1 is about honest observation: what kinds of tasks are you using AI for, and which of those could be handed off entirely?

### Week 2 — First Delegation
Delegation thinking isn't a theory — it's a practice. Week 2 has you pick one recurring task from your audit, design a simple agent workflow for it, and test it against reality. This is where the mental shift from "what do I type?" to "what do I hand off?" becomes concrete.

### Week 3 — Tiny Pipeline
The orchestration leap. You take two tasks that normally happen in sequence and wire them together so the second one starts automatically when the first one finishes. This is the moment you stop being the person who passes the baton — the system does it. And it takes less than 10 minutes to build your first one.

---

## How to Use This Section

1. **Do Week 1 first.** The audit is your baseline. Don't skip it.
2. **Do one exercise per week.** Let the habit sink in between sessions.
3. **Keep notes.** A scratch file or a notebook page is fine. You'll want to see your own progress.
4. **When you finish Week 3, loop back.** Run the audit again. You'll be surprised at how much has changed.

---

## Before You Start

You need:
- Access to an AI chat tool (ChatGPT, Claude, or anything similar)
- Your chat history from the last week or so
- 10 uninterrupted minutes per week
- A willingness to be honest about what you find

That's it. No API keys. No coding. No special tools. The exercises work with whatever AI you already use.
