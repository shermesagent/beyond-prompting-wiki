# Reverse-Centaur

## What It Is

A centaur is a system where the human and AI each do what they do best — the classic framing from chess where human judgment guides AI computation. A **Reverse-Centaur** is the opposite: the AI makes the judgments and the human does the machine work (feeding inputs, copy-pasting between steps, formatting outputs). The human becomes a connector between AI steps rather than the director.

The term comes from Cory Doctorow's 2026 book *The Reverse-Centaur's Guide to Life After AI*.

## Why It Matters

The Reverse-Centaur is the single most useful diagnostic for whether your AI workflow is actually making you better — or just busier. The same technology, deployed two different ways, produces opposite outcomes:

- **Centaur:** You decide. AI executes. Your agency grows.
- **Reverse-Centaur:** AI decides. You execute. Your agency shrinks.

This is not about whether AI is good or bad. It's about who does what in the workflow. And the difference is entirely in the design.

## The Simple Test

Ask these three questions about any pipeline, template, or delegation you've set up:

1. **Who makes the decisions?** — You, or the AI?
2. **Who does the repetitive work?** — You, or the AI?
3. **After this workflow runs, do you feel more capable or less?** — This is the gut check. Your body knows before your brain does.

If you're making the decisions and the AI is doing the repetitive work: Centaur. If the AI is making the decisions and you're doing the repetitive work: Reverse-Centaur.

## The Research Behind the Metaphor

Two studies from July 2026 add empirical weight to what the metaphor captures:

### Friction That's Worth Keeping

Researchers studying AI-assisted structural design (arXiv:2607.07521) found that removing ALL friction from the creative process is counterproductive. They distinguished between:

- **Repetitive modelling friction** — the boring stuff. Redrawing, reformatting, recalculating. This should be automated.
- **Reflective design friction** — the productive struggle of iterating through constraints. This is where judgment lives and should be preserved.

A Reverse-Centaur workflow removes reflective friction — the AI does the thinking while the human does the typing. A Centaur workflow removes only repetitive friction and keeps the reflective kind.

### The Cognitive Outsourcing Trap

A controlled study of human-AI co-creativity (arXiv:2607.07522) found that people who self-reported higher cognitive outsourcing ("I let the other person do the thinking") produced less original ideas — not just with AI partners, but even when working with another human. Delegation that becomes cognitive outsourcing shrinks your creative capacity regardless of who you're working with.

## Common Reverse-Centaur Patterns

You might be in Reverse-Centaur territory if:

- You spend more time proofreading and formatting AI output than making decisions about it
- Your "pipelines" are really just you copying and pasting between different AI chat windows
- The AI decides what matters about the task and you fill in the blanks
- You can't explain why the AI made the decisions it did — you just accepted the output
- After your "delegation" session, you feel drained rather than relieved

## The Fix

Three things that reverse a Reverse-Centaur:

1. **Move the repetitive work to the AI side.** If you're formatting, copying, pasting, or retyping — ask whether the AI could do that part instead. Collapse handoffs so fewer steps require human intervention.

2. **Add a thinking checkpoint.** After each pipeline step, ask yourself: "Is this going in the right direction?" 30 seconds. That's the reflective friction you're keeping. The pipeline handles the repetition. You handle the judgment.

3. **Check your engagement.** Before delegating, write down what you think the answer might be. One sentence. Then compare it to the AI's output. If you've stopped making guesses at all, you've outsourced your thinking — and that's the Reverse-Centaur's last stage.
