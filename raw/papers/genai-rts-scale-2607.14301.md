# Measuring How Students Rely on Generative AI in Academic Writing: Development and Multi-Source Validation of the Generative AI Reliance Types Scale (GenAI-RTS)

**arXiv:** 2607.14301
**Category:** cs.AI (cross-listed from cs.HC)

## Abstract

This paper presents the development and multi-source validation of the Generative AI Reliance Types Scale (GenAI-RTS), a validated instrument that measures four distinct types of reliance on generative AI in academic writing:

1. **Strategic Reliance** — Using AI deliberately as one tool among many, with clear boundaries and intentional choices about when and how to engage it.

2. **Instrumental Reliance** — Using AI as a productivity tool to complete tasks faster or with less effort, without necessarily changing the nature of the work.

3. **Dependent Reliance** — Relying on AI in ways that reduce the user's own skill development or independent capability over time.

4. **Dialogic Reliance** — Using AI as a thinking partner in an iterative, exploratory conversation that shapes the direction of the work.

The scale was validated across multiple sources, providing a reliable self-assessment tool for understanding *how* someone relies on AI — not just how much.

## Key Implications

The four reliance types form a spectrum from healthy to concerning:
- **Dialogic → Strategic → Instrumental → Dependent** (roughly from most growth-oriented to most substitution-oriented)

Understanding one's reliance type is the first step toward calibrating AI use. The scale makes this measurable rather than subjective.
