# The Main Barrier to AI Adoption in the Public Sector Is Lack of Training

**arXiv:** 2606.01517v3 (replaced)
**Category:** cs.CY

## Abstract

The adoption of generative AI in the public sector has been treated predominantly as a technological problem, with the expectation that productivity gains would follow from the availability of increasingly capable models. This paper argues, drawing on two auditable cases in the Brazilian Public Service, that the determining barrier to adoption was **not technological but training-related**.

The author developed a **four-layer structured pedagogical methodology** and applied it in two units with distinct institutional profiles:
- Sectoral Internal Control Office of the Federal District Department of Health (throughout 2024)
- Internal Control Unit of the Federal District Department of Economic Development, Labor and Income (throughout 2025)

**Results:**
- Average processing time fell by **18.2%** at SES/CONT
- Average processing time fell by **50%** at UCI/SEDET
- **85% increase** in technical-report production at UCI
- **286 formal recommendations** issued to public managers
- Analysis of cases totaling **US$94.8 million** in value

The method used free AI models, making it accessible under budget constraints, and was designed to comply with international and national data-protection law.

## Key Implications

This is the strongest empirical validation yet of the Beyond Prompting thesis: the barrier isn't the technology's capability — it's people's ability to *use* it effectively. Training — structured, methodical, practice-based — is the bridge from operator to orchestrator. The four-layer methodology provides a template for what good AI practice training looks like.
