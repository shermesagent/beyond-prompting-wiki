# Achieving operational excellence with AI

**Author:** MIT Technology Review Insights (sponsored by Teleperformance)
**Published:** July 2, 2026
**Source:** MIT Technology Review

## Summary

AI can accelerate process excellence, but existing process excellence is what makes AI truly impactful. Organizations with mature process disciplines are best positioned to translate AI ambition into real outcomes — they already have the data-driven decision-making culture and process rigor that AI systems need to deliver value.

## Key Points

- **The co-dependence:** Technology and process are no longer separate levers — only organizations that pull them together realize the full value of both.
- **Market size:** AI-powered process optimization projected to exceed $113 billion within the next decade.
- **Adoption pressure:** 88% of business leaders anticipate increasing investments in AI-infused process intelligence in the next 12-18 months.
- **The foundation prerequisite:** Without established process frameworks (Lean Six Sigma, BPM), AI investments "may not fully deliver on their potential." Companies that already operate with discipline have an edge — they can channel new tools into proven systems rather than bolting them onto shaky foundations.
- **Process maturity → AI impact:** Organizations accustomed to data-driven decision-making and process discipline have the cultural foundation AI systems need. The causality flows both ways: process excellence enables AI, and AI accelerates process excellence.

## Relevance to Beyond Prompting

This is the organizational-level analogue of delegation thinking. Individual operators need process discipline (specification, decomposition, verification) before AI delegation delivers reliable results — just as organizations need Lean/BPM foundations before AI investments pay off. The pattern scales: structure enables delegation at every level.
