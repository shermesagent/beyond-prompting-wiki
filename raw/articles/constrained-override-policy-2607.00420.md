# A Simple Solution to Improving Human Supervision of Algorithms: Evidence from Smart Vending

**Authors:** Zhao, Han, Chen & Zhu
**Date:** July 2, 2026
**Venue:** arXiv:2607.00420

## Key Findings

Field experiment with 553 workers at a major Chinese smart vending machine retailer (59,000+ machines, 4,000+ products). Tested three override policies for human-AI collaboration:

1. **No override** — AI decides everything autonomously
2. **Free override** — workers can adjust any AI recommendation
3. **Constrained override** — workers get exactly 2 overrides per machine per day

**Results:** Free overrides reduced inventory by 1.95% but also reduced sales by 1.19% — workers over-corrected and introduced bias. Constrained overrides reduced inventory by 1.28% *without* hurting sales. The constraint forced workers to be selective about when their private knowledge genuinely added value. Experienced workers, high-value products, and growth-stage products benefited most. A simulated personalized constrained policy boosted sales probability by 9.1%.

**Core insight:** Giving people unlimited freedom to override AI is worse than no freedom at all. A constrained number of overrides (2 per decision episode) makes human judgment *more* valuable by forcing it to be selective.
