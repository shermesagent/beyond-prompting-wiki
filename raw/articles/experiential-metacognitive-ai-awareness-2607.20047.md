# Experiential Versus Instructional Approaches for Eliciting Metacognitive Awareness in AI-Assisted Learning

arXiv:2607.20047, July 23, 2026

With generative AI entering classrooms, the question of which teaching approach best supports metacognitive skill acquisition in AI-assisted learning becomes pressing. This short-term longitudinal study investigates two contrasting approaches: experiential learning (hands-on) and instructional learning (classical lectures).

A quasi-experiment with 126 university students from a first-year engineering course, distributed across the two conditions, completed a two-hour session on learning with GenAI in the corresponding learning style. Metacognitive awareness (knowledge of cognition + regulation of cognition) was measured before and after, and again at five weeks.

Key findings:
- Experiential methods outperform instructional approaches in engagement and knowledge of cognition immediately after intervention
- By five weeks, groups converged on knowledge measures
- Experiential group showed a delayed, continuous within-group increase in regulation of cognition NOT observed in the instructional group
- Knowledge and regulation develop on different timescales under experiential learning
- Hands-on use beats lectures for building lasting metacognitive awareness about AI
