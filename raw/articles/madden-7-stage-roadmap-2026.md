# Brian Madden / Citrix — "The 7-stage roadmap for human-AI collaboration (2026 Edition)"

**Author:** Brian Madden, VP & Futurist at Citrix
**Published:** June 10, 2026
**Personal site:** https://bmad.com/thinking

## Core Thesis

"The real AI transformation is happening worker-by-worker, not top-down. Corporate-led AI initiatives consistently fail to deliver measurable value, while individual workers using consumer AI tools (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) are quietly transforming how knowledge work gets done. This is not shadow IT to be contained — it is shadow strategy to be enabled."

Workers have access to the "invisible 80%" of their own work — tacit knowledge, judgment, patterns, and context that no external observer can see. Enterprise AI programs fail because they can't see this 80%. Workers succeed because they live inside it.

## The 7-Stage Roadmap

The roadmap describes how individual workers adopt AI over time. Key framing from bmad.com: **"It's not a ladder, it's a palace, with the second brain as the permanent foundation."** The stages represent deepening integration, not linear progression.

## The Cognitive Stack

Five layers from human intent to mechanical execution:

1. **The Worker** — States intent and exercises judgment. Decides what matters, what's urgent, what the goal actually is.
2. **The Cognitive Extension ("the brain")** — The thing you actually talk to. Holds your full context: participants, preferred format, what's sensitive, what happened last meeting. Plans the approach and sequences.
3. **AI Agents ("the claws")** — Purpose-built digital workers that execute tasks. Karpathy's concept: personal AI agents as appendages that serve the brain.
4. **Tools** — APIs, databases, file systems.
5. **Execution** — The actual work getting done.

## Key Insight: Workspace as Control Plane

"The enterprise response should not be to block, restrict, or replace worker-chosen tools with inferior 'approved' alternatives. Instead, enterprises should secure the environment where work happens, not the specific tools workers use. The workspace becomes the control plane — the governance boundary where apps, identity, security, and context converge around the worker."

## Relevance to Beyond Prompting

The 7-stage roadmap directly maps to the operator→orchestrator→architect progression. Stage 1 (one-off prompts) = Operator. Middle stages (second brain, co-pilot) = Orchestrator. Late stages (agents, autonomous systems) = Architect. The "palace, not a ladder" framing is crucial: the second brain stage isn't something you pass through — it becomes permanent infrastructure.

The Cognitive Stack also provides a vocabulary for what happens structurally when you move beyond prompting: the "brain" layer (memory, context, plans) sits between you and the AI agents, handling what prompts currently do manually.
