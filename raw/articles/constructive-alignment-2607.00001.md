# Constructive Alignment: Governing Preference Dynamics in Human-AI Interaction

**Authors:** Kanwal et al.
**Date:** July 2, 2026
**Venue:** AAAI-26 Workshop on Machine Ethics (arXiv:2607.00001)

## Key Findings

Theoretical framework arguing that AI alignment is not just about making AI follow static human preferences — it's about recognizing and governing how AI *shapes* human preferences over time.

The authors propose "Constructive Alignment": a framework for regulating how AI systems influence the evolution of human preferences through repeated interaction. Every AI interaction subtly adjusts what users attend to, value, and endorse.

**Core insight:** The practice of working with AI isn't just about getting good outputs. It's about noticing how the tool is changing you — what you consider normal, what you consider your job, and what you consider worth doing yourself. This metacognitive layer is essential to the beyond-prompting practice.
