# Cognitive Offloading, Atrophy Risk, and the Design of Human-AI Interfaces

**Source:** Kennesaw State University, Cognitive Science Proceedings
**Date:** May 2, 2026

## Key Findings

This paper offers four high-impact research questions to guide an interdisciplinary scientific program spanning cognitive science, human-computer interaction, and AI design.

**Core argument:** Cognitive load theory suggests that well-designed AI interfaces should selectively introduce friction, increasing germane cognitive load, rather than eliminating all friction.

**Key concepts:**
- **Frictionless interfaces** (chat, single-click) maximize cognitive offloading — which feels good in the moment but risks skill atrophy
- **Strategic friction** preserves the cognitive engagement needed to maintain expertise
- The goal is not to make AI harder to use, but to make it harder to use *without thinking*

## Relevance to Beyond Prompting

This research directly supports the orchestrator mindset: the orchestrator doesn't surrender thinking to the AI — they design checkpoints, verifications, and moments of deliberate engagement into their workflow. Frictionless prompting is the operator's trap. Strategic friction is the orchestrator's discipline.
