# Accurate but Not Confident or Confident but Not Accurate

**Source:** ACM Digital Library
**Date:** April 13, 2026

## Key Finding

This study provides the first empirical evidence that AI support, while enhancing performance, can impair users' confidence calibration — their ability to accurately judge how well they're performing.

**The paradox:** People using AI get better results but worse at knowing when they're right or wrong. They become either overconfident (trusting AI output that's wrong) or underconfident (doubting their own correct judgment when AI disagrees).

## Relevance to Beyond Prompting

This directly validates the Trust Calibration concept in the wiki. Moving beyond prompting requires *better* calibration, not worse. The orchestrator builds verification checkpoints specifically because AI tools degrade our natural calibration.
