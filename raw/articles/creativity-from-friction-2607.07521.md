# Creativity from Friction: Human-AI Interaction for Exploratory Structural Design

**Authors:** Anonymous (2026)
**arXiv ID:** 2607.07521
**Published:** July 7, 2026

## Abstract

AI agents that generate final answers based on user input often do not meet the needs of creative fields. Fields such as structural design and architecture need interactive systems that help users externalise and develop ideas, explore alternatives, and refine partial solutions. The final product of such designs needs to comply with many constraints concerning spatial configuration, mechanical behaviour, material quantities, and costs. These constraints create friction in the design process, which can stimulate novel and creative solutions. This paper discusses the misalignment between current generative AI goals to remove friction and provide final solutions and the needs of creators, such as structural designers, who develop ideas through iterative work. It presents design dimensions of systems allowing for constrained human-AI co-creation that rely on vision-language models making structural exploration conversational, multimodal, and responsive to evolving human intent. Through a pilot design interface and a study with experts, the paper shows how structural designers perceive interactive AI systems and how such systems can support design space exploration by reducing repetitive modelling friction while preserving reflective design friction.

## Key Findings

- Removing ALL friction is counterproductive for creative work
- "Reflective design friction" — the productive struggle of iterating through constraints — is essential
- AI should reduce repetitive modelling friction while preserving reflective design friction
- Creators need interactive systems for externalizing ideas, exploring alternatives, and refining partial solutions — not final answers
