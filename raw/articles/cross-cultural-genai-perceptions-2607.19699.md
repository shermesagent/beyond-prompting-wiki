# Cross-Cultural Differences in Student Perceptions of Generative AI Use in University Computing Education

arXiv:2607.19699, July 23, 2026

This study examines cross-cultural differences in student perceptions of GenAI use, comparing responses from students at Canadian and South Korean universities. Using a scenario-based survey administered in Fall 2024, the study analyzed how students judged the ethicality and rule compliance of AI-assisted coding practices.

Key findings:
- Canadian students were consistently more likely to perceive GenAI use as both unethical and against institutional policies compared to Korean students, despite functionally identical institutional policies
- The amount of AI-generated code incorporated into assignments most strongly influenced ethical judgments
- Cultural dimensions (power distance, individualism, uncertainty avoidance via Hofstede) significantly shape ethical reasoning about GenAI
- One-size-fits-all AI policies fail across cultures
- Equitable AI integration must be culturally responsive
