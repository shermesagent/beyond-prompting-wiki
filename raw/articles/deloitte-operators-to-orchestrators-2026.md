# Deloitte 2026 Global Technology Leadership Study — "From Operators to Orchestrators"

**Published:** April 30, 2026
**Source:** Deloitte LLP via PRNewswire
**Methodology:** Online survey of 660+ senior technology executives, December 2025 to February 2026

## Key Findings

The role of the technology leader is undergoing a profound transformation. Today's tech leaders are not only expected to run technology, but to shape strategy, lead change, build AI-ready teams, and drive enterprise-wide outcomes.

The research revealed **three shifts** redefining technology leadership:

### 1. The Value Mandate: From Uptime to Outcomes
Technology's role has evolved from enabling the business to shaping its strategy. Leaders are now expected to deliver measurable enterprise value across growth, productivity, and customer impact. **79% of leaders** report this is their top priority. However, **42% report low or no ROI on AI investments**, demonstrating the difficulty of delivering on this new value mandate.

### 2. The Capability Gap: Ambition vs. Reality
A stark paradox between leaders' confidence and organizational reality. As expectations rise, many organizations are still catching up on foundational data, talent, and operating models. Challenges to scaling AI are not the technology itself, but internal constraints like poor data quality, security concerns, talent shortages, and legacy systems.

### 3. The Resource Squeeze: More Mandate, Same Model
Tech leaders are expected to **run, change, protect, and grow** the business simultaneously — within funding models and governance structures that have not kept pace. Tech spending projected to rise only modestly; **41% of tech leaders** report the business sees them as unable to keep up with demand.

## Key Quotes

"The era of the operational technologist is over. This shift has been building for over a decade, and AI is the catalyst bringing that into focus. Today's CIO isn't just leading technology; they are being asked to redesign the very fabric of how the business runs. As operating models and investments catch up to this new reality, success will be defined by judgment and trade-offs."

"Today's leaders are defined by their ability to orchestrate across the C-suite and translate technology into measurable outcomes. Those who remain focused solely on delivering underlying systems risk being sidelined, while those who embrace this moment can lead their organizations into the future."

## Relevance to Beyond Prompting

The study explicitly uses "From Operators to Orchestrators" as its framing — the exact language of the beyond-prompting shift. The findings mirror the wiki's core thesis: technical execution alone is not enough; the value comes from orchestrating across systems, building AI-ready capabilities, and exercising judgment at the right level.
