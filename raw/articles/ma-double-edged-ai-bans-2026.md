# The Double-edged Effect of Banning Generative AI on Online Q&A Communities: Evidence from Stack Exchange

**Authors:** Yuanhong Ma, Qinglai He, Xitong Li, Lynn Wu
**Date:** July 7, 2026
**Source:** arXiv:2607.04601 (cs.CY)

## Abstract

The authors investigate how banning generative AI-generated content (AIGC) affects knowledge seeking, knowledge contribution, and contribution efficiency in online Q&A communities. After the launch of ChatGPT in late November 2022, several Stack Exchange communities implemented official bans on AIGC over concerns such as less reliable and socially engaged content.

Using a difference-in-differences approach, they find a double-edged impact: while the AIGC ban increases knowledge seeking (higher volume of posted questions), it simultaneously reduces contribution efficiency (lower proportion of questions receiving satisfactory answers within the expected time frame). These impacts are only evident in non-STEM communities.

The mechanism exploration reveals that the AIGC ban spurs question volume in topics where AIGC is less reliable and where social interaction is highly expected. In contrast, the ban hampers answer efficiency in communities where LLMs are capable of producing reliable answers and where social interactivity is minimal.

Additionally, the results indicate increased human involvement from knowledge seekers and contributors following the ban. They adapt their behavior by posting questions and answers that are more informationally rich and socially engaging.

## Key Findings

- **Banning AI has a double edge:** More human questions asked (good), but fewer get satisfactory answers (bad)
- **Effect is domain-specific:** Only in non-STEM communities. STEM communities, where AI answers are more reliable, suffer more from the ban
- **Human adaptation:** After the ban, people post richer, more socially engaging content — they try harder
- **The tension:** AI bans protect quality but reduce throughput. The orchestrator's question isn't "ban or allow?" It's "when and where?"
