# You Shall Not Pass! Where and Why Developers Draw The Line on AI Autonomy

**Authors:** Choudhuri, Bird, Badea, Gerosa & Sarma
**Date:** July 2, 2026
**Venue:** arXiv:2607.00533

## Key Findings

Mixed-methods study surveying 448 professional software developers at Microsoft about where they draw the line on AI autonomy in their daily work.

**What people accept:** Most developers accept AI producing work *under their oversight.*

**Where acceptance drops:** Three types of work resist delegation:
- **Identity-defining tasks** — "this is who I am as a professional"
- **Human-facing tasks** — "someone else will see this and judge me for it"
- **Design-oriented tasks** — "this requires taste, not just correctness"

**Factors predicting willingness to delegate:**
- AI experience (more = more willing)
- Risk tolerance (higher = more willing)
- Task demands (harder tasks = more willing to accept AI help)

**Factors predicting reluctance:**
- Accountability ("if this goes wrong, I'm responsible")
- Task identity ("this task is part of who I am professionally")

**Core insight:** Delegation boundaries follow a predictable pattern. Knowing where your lines are and *why* is the foundation of intentional delegation practice — not a reason to stop, but a map of where to start.
