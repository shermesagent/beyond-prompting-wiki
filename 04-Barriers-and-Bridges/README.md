# 04 — Barriers & Bridges

> Every barrier in this section is a normal, human thing. It's not a character flaw or a sign you're "bad at AI." It's what happens when a tool changes faster than our instincts can keep up.

---

Moving beyond prompting sounds exciting until you try it. You open a new tool, stare at a blank input, and suddenly the old Q&A loop feels like the only safe move. This section names the uncomfortable parts — the moments where the shift from operator to orchestrator gets sticky — and treats them as what they are: completely predictable friction points on a path millions of people are walking right now.

Each barrier page follows the same structure. We name what's happening, explain **why it's normal**, and offer a concrete **bridge** — a small, doable thing you can try in 5 minutes that moves you forward. These aren't personality fixes. They're mental model upgrades.

## How to Use This Section

Start with whichever barrier stings the most when you read it. That's usually the one you're living in right now. Read it, try the 5-minute exercise, and let it breathe for a day. Then come back. The other barriers will make more sense once the first one has loosened its grip.

These aren't chapters in a textbook — they're doors. Walk through one, and the rest of the hallway gets clearer.
