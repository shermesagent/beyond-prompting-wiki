## Summary

A case study of an AI education session with 54 adults (48 in-person, 6 virtual) in a predominantly African American community on the east side of a major Midwestern city. The session was co-designed with community partners.

## Key Findings

- Concerns about AI persisted but **shifted to specific, locally grounded questions** about AI design and deployment — not abstract fears
- The research argues for AI literacy frameworks grounded in local community contexts that strengthen community capacity
- Adults outside higher education receive less attention in AI education, despite being most affected by AI-driven changes in the workforce

## Why It Matters

The study shows what real-world AI adoption looks like when it's community-centered rather than top-down. The shift from abstract fears to specific, grounded questions is exactly the pattern the beyond-prompting wiki advocates: stop worrying about "AI" in general and start asking "what should I delegate, and what should I keep?" The community co-design model is a template for any organization bringing its people through the operator→orchestrator shift.
