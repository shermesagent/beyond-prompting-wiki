## Summary

A Clinical and Translational Science Award (CTSA) program built a human-in-the-loop AI agent that assembles dossiers of sourced evidence for scholars and drafts one-sentence impact summaries for staff review. This wasn't a simulation — it was a real deployment evaluated in an actual impact-reporting workflow.

## Key Findings

- **81.7% usable rate** — both reviewers accepted or edited 81.7% of the agent's findings
- **Staff time savings:** median 14 minutes per scholar, replacing an estimated 15 hours of manual assembly
- **Evaluation:** 10 career-development (KL2/K12) scholars, 507 findings coded by two independent evaluation staff
- **Inter-rater agreement:** moderate (Cohen's kappa 0.43 on usable-versus-reject)
- The agent's recall was close to human-level in a profile discovery study

## Why It Matters

This is a real-world pipeline that works — not a demo, not a proof of concept. The human-in-the-loop design (agent assembles, staff reviews) produced usable output 81.7% of the time while cutting labor from 15 hours to 14 minutes per scholar. It's the review-first pattern in a production healthcare setting with verifiable metrics.
